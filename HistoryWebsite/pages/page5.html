First Meeting of the Belize-Guatemala Joint Commission, 2014

Event Details

A significant event in 2014 regarding the Belize-Guatemala territorial dispute was the establishment of a formal framework for ongoing dialogue and cooperation. This occurred with the First Meeting of the Belize-Guatemala Joint Commission, which took place on February 10th, 2014. The Joint Commission served as a crucial platform for both nations to discuss various aspects of the dispute and explore potential solutions. It represented a shift from ad-hoc meetings to a more structured and sustained mechanism for addressing the long-standing issue.

Here are some additional details about the Joint Commission's significance:

Facilitated Dialogue: The Joint Commission provided a dedicated space for regular communication and negotiation between Belize and Guatemala. Expanded Focus: It aimed to go beyond the single issue of the territorial dispute and explore broader areas of cooperation like trade, security, and cultural exchange. Potential for Progress: The establishment of the Joint Commission offered a promising avenue for building trust and finding common ground towards a peaceful and lasting resolution.
